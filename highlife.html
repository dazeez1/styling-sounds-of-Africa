Description

Highlife is a music genre that originated in Ghana in the early 20th century. It combines traditional African rhythms with Western instruments like guitars and horns, creating a distinctive and harmonic sound.

History

Highlife became popular in the 1920s and 1930s, spreading across West Africa. The genre has evolved over the decades, with artists like E.T. Mensah and Osibisa bringing it to international audiences.

Key Characteristics

  • Use of guitars, horns, and percussion instruments
  • Smooth and melodic vocal lines
  • Influences from jazz and swing
